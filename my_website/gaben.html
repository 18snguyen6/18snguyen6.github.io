Gabe Logan "Lord Gaben" Newell

  • Lifespan: November 3, 1962 - Present
  • Education: 3 years at Harvard (Dropout)
  • Occupation: Programmer, Businessman, and President of Valve Corporation
  • Quote: "The programmers of tomorrow are the wizards of the future. You're gonna look like you have magic powers compared to everyone else."

The Life of Gabe Logan Newell

1962

  • Birth of Gabe Logan Newell

    Gabe was born on November 3, 1962 in Seattle, Washington.

1980s

  • University Education

    Gaben attended Harvard University for three years before dropping out to go work for Microsoft.

  • Microsoft Producer

    Gaben then spent the next 13 years working at Microsoft as a producer for the Windows 1.01, 1.02, and 1.03 OS. He mentions that learned more from 3 months at Microsoft than his entire time at Harvard.

    • 1990s

    • Inspirations for the Gaming Industry

      Gaben's first experience with video games was Star Trek on the Burroughs mainframe. Later, Gaben saw Doom and realized that video games would be the future of entertainment. Lastly, Mario 64, Gaben's favorite game of all time, made him see video games as a form of art.

  • 1996: Birth of Valve Corporation

    After his experiences with the previous games, Gaben left Microsoft and founded Valve Corporation. Gaben's first contribution would be his expansion to PC gaming with hits such as Half-Life, Counter-Strike, and Team Fortress. Gaben saw that the PC was a better platform for gaming than the NES or Sega systems.

    • 2000s

    • Steam

      Gaben's next contribution would be the release of Steam in 2003. Steam's original purpose was to automate the update process of Valve games. However, as the years progressed, Steam transitioned into becoming a platform that sold video games and held all the games in a digital library. After that, Valve introduced Steam Workshop which allowed for users and developers to modify the game. In 2015, Steam added an online streaming system where a user can stream his or her gameplay to anyone. Overall, Steam evolved into a very convenient and massive gaming platform that is popular. It controls about 70% of the $4 billion market for downloaded PC games.

  • Fuchs' Dystrophy

    Gaben suffered from Fuch's dystrophy, a disease which affects the cornea. However, Gaben had done two cornea transplants in 2006 and 2007 which had cured him and changed him completely.

    • Present

    • Gaben

      Gaben has become a popular figure within the PC gaming community. He is most famously known for his Steam sales and is commonly referred to as "Lord Gaben." These sales appear very often, usually over summer, winter and the weekends.

    Steam: 2002 vs Present

    Steam started off as a program that was used to update Valve games. Throughout the years, Steam progressed into a distribution company that sold games not only from Valve but also from other producers. Now, Steam is a huge distribution company contains its own virtual economy, vast amount of games, and conveniences of PC gaming in one simple software.

    Gaben's Favorite Games

    Super Mario 64 was a game that Gaben remembers very clearly from its level designs and it appearing as an artwork as a whole. Doom was what made Gaben realize the power of using the PC as a platform for gaming which led to the creation of Valve Corporation. Star Trek was Gaben's favorite game that he played during his highschool years.
